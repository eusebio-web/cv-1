Research interests

Synopsis:

The focus of my current research is in the multi-disciplinary integration of typically intercollegiate emerging technologies with industrial related applications. My background in Mechanical Engineering, with specialist postgraduate and postdoctoral proficiency in Noise, Vibration, and Harshness Engineering has given me a solid numerate background to investigate the wide range of physical behaviours of individual components and complex system. A typical example would be the specific re-design of the housing for the air-bag deployment sensor in a Bugatti EB110 supercar, and on the other hand the complete development of a new motor-vehicle for Daewoo to target the USA market. Such vehicle development can similarly be applied to the design, development and build of High Speed Railway vehicles, railway tracks, and foundations for noise, vibration, and harshness. My solid background in engineering enables me reconcile results from theoretical models with practical measurements on physical systems.

My postgraduate studies on Geographical Information Management (GIS) gave me the skills to integrate technological information applicable to individual components with those applicable to groups of components forming complex systems. Geography for instance enables the traversal from local scales to global scales, traversing from say a building information model (BIM) to a precinct information model (PIM). Such concepts are pertinent in integrating systems. When a complex system like a railway network is put to use in an urban setting, the sustainability of such a system is impinged on by the integration of relevant technologies. My second PhD research developed a framework for the analytics of big-data from consumers of digital services. The veracity of such big-data facilitates identifying the relationship between drivers of mobility choice, passenger behaviour on the railway network (at individual, local, and global scales), socio-demography, and urban morphology. This also enables relationships to be established between operational in-service issues like crowding in trains, delays, scheduling, and design issues like structural vehicle noise, vibration, and ride resulting from component design and integration. Efficiencies are uncovered by BIM concepts which link all disciplines involved over time in project planning, designs, operation and management, and end-of-life. These apply to the whole-life-cycle costs and sustainability of the infrastructure.

At this stage of my career I am interested in developing technology tools useful to operators of mobility (railways, etc.) networks for the management, maintenance and interventions assessment. I am interested in the application of emerging engineering technologies to the design and development of automobiles and high speed railway vehicles. I am interested in the application of philosophies of GIS and BIM, enabling the traversal from small scale to large scale, integrating multiple disciplines in a project across time-scales. My broad academic and working experience facilitates the application of my research to a wide range of physical scenarios. I could function at the height of proficiency in related fields of engineering, transportation, logistics, environmental sciences, social sciences, big-data analytics, and computing.
